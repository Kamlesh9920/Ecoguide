Best Practices for Waste Sorting

Essential tips for effective waste segregation at home

Clean Before Sorting

Rinse containers and remove food residue to prevent contamination and ensure proper recycling.

Use Clear Labels

Label your bins clearly with both text and color coding to help all family members sort correctly.

Check Local Guidelines

Recycling rules vary by location. Always verify what your local waste management system accepts.

Prepare Separate Bins

Label bins clearly to avoid confusion. Use color-coded bins for easy identification.

Green Bin: Organic Waste

Blue Bin: Recyclables

Red Bin: Non-Recyclables

Yellow Bin: Hazardous Waste

Educate Family Members

Place a segregation chart near the bins to remind everyone what goes where. This fosters habit formation and teamwork.

Compost Organic Waste

Set up a compost bin to turn organic waste into nutrient-rich compost for your garden, reducing methane emissions from landfills.

Waste Segregation Rules

Mandatory Requirements

  • Separate wet waste (organic) from dry waste (recyclables)
  • Store hazardous waste separately in designated containers
  • Use appropriate colored bins as per local guidelines
  • Clean recyclables before disposal to prevent contamination

Best Practices

  • Compost organic waste at home when possible
  • Label bins clearly for family members and domestic help
  • Regular cleaning and maintenance of waste storage areas
  • Participate in community awareness programs

Mission

We're dedicated to creating a sustainable future through education, community engagement, and practical waste management solutions that make a real difference.

Our Story

Founded in 2020 by environmental scientist Dr. Sarah Johnson, our organization emerged from a simple observation: most households want to manage their waste responsibly but lack the knowledge and tools to do so effectively.

What started as weekend workshops in local community centers has grown into a nationwide movement, reaching over 100,000 households across 15 cities. Our approach combines scientific, expertise with practical, everyday solutions that families can implement immediately.
